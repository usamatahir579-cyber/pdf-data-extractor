•Tools and resources(page 11)Introduction
•Black holes represent the most mysterious and extreme prediction of general 
relativity—regions where gravity become so intense that not even light can 
escape. In 1974, Stephen hawking introduced the concept of hawking radiation, 
showing that black hole are not entirely black but emit radiation due to quantum 
effects near the event horizon.
•This discovery created a profound conflict between quantum mechanics, which 
insist that information conserved, and general relativity, which seems to allow 
information to vanish inside black hole.•This conflict is known as black hole information paradox —one of the most 
significant unsolved problems in theoretical physics.
•Understanding where information is truly lost in black hole or somehow 
preserved in subtle ways has far -reaching implications for the unification of 
quantum mechanics and gravity(general relativity), and for our understanding of 
reality itself.Objectives
•The primary objectives of this project are:
•To study thermodynamic properties of black hole (temperature, entropy, and 
radiation).
•To understand the origin and formulation of the black hole information paradox.
•To explore proposed resolution such as black hole complementarity, holographic 
principle, and quantum information theory.
•To construct a conceptual and mathematical framework explaining how 
information may be conserved during black hole evaporation.Methodology
•Since this is a theoretical research project, the work will focus on analytical study and 
Literature based exploration supported by numerical visualization.
•Literature review:
•Study original paper by Stephen hawking Jacob Bekenstein , and Leonard
•Susskind.
•Review modern research on holography and quantum gravity perspective.
•Analytical study:
•Analyze the black hole thermodynamics and quantum field effect near horizon.
•Explore equations governing hawking radiation and entropy.
•Discuss quantum mechanical properties of information conservation..
•Comparative analysis:
•Comparing classical, semi classical, and quantum gravity approaches to the 
paradox.
•Evaluate the strength and weakness of different proposed solution.
•Simulation and visualization:
•Generate visual plots to represent evaporation and information flow concepts.Expected outcomes
•A comprehensive theoretical report explaining the nature of the black hole 
information paradox.
•A clear comparison between classical, quantum, and holographic interpretations.
•Graphs or visualizations showing relationships between entropy, temperature, 
and mass.
•A research paper suitable for an undergraduate physics journal.
•Personal mastery of fundamental concepts in thermodynamics, relativity, and 
quantum theory, laying a strong foundation for graduate -level research.Significance of the project
•This research connects the three greatest pillars of modern physics —quantum 
mechanics, thermodynamics, and general relativity.
•Resolving or even deeply understanding the black hole information paradox is 
considered a key step toward a theory of quantum gravity, which aims to describe 
the universe at its most fundamental level.
•For a developing scientist, this project provides the opportunity to think like a 
theorist—questioning assumptions, connecting ideas, and researching toward the 
ultimate truths of nature.  Tools and resources
